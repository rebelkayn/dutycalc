The problem

The three silent leaks
draining your margin.

These aren't edge cases. They happen on virtually every shipment, in every industry, regardless of who's in office. Your broker doesn't catch them because they're incentivized for speed — not savings.

LEAK 01

The "Safety" Tax

When documentation is vague, brokers pick the higher-duty code because it's less likely to trigger a CBP audit. They're protecting themselves — not your margin.

Real scenario

An industrial sensor has two valid codes: "General Electronics" at 5% and "Scientific Instruments" at 0%. The broker picks 5% because it's "safer." Across 1,000 shipments, that's 5% of your total import value — gone.

LEAK 02

Unused Free Trade Agreements

Research shows FTAs are utilized in only 40–60% of cases where they actually apply. Missing paperwork means you pay full MFN rates for no reason.

Real scenario

Your Mexican supplier forgot the USMCA origin statement. Your broker sees no statement, defaults to the MFN rate of 4.5% instead of 0%. They don't call you — they just process and move on.

LEAK 03

Valuation Errors

Most importers don't realize they can legally deduct inland freight, tooling assists, and other non-dutiable costs from their taxable value.

Real scenario

Your $100K invoice includes $5,000 of inland freight (factory to port). If your broker doesn't strip it out, you pay a 10% tariff on a truck ride through a foreign country. That's $500 per shipment.
