Tariff rates, HTS classifications, trade remedy orders, and applicable surcharges are subject to frequent modification by the U.S. government and foreign governments, often with minimal advance notice.

7.3 HTS Classification Complexity

The classification of goods under the Harmonized Tariff Schedule is a complex legal determination governed by the General Rules of Interpretation (GRI), the Additional U.S. Rules of Interpretation, Section Notes, Chapter Notes, and a substantial body of administrative rulings and judicial precedent from the U.S. Court of International Trade and the U.S. Court of Appeals for the Federal Circuit. Automated classification tools, including AI-powered classifiers, cannot substitute for the professional judgment required by this analysis. Classification errors can result in substantial penalties, including under 19 U.S.C. § 1592 (penalties for fraud, gross negligence, or negligence).

7.4 Reasonable Care

Under 19 U.S.C. § 1484, importers of record are required to exercise "reasonable care" in classifying merchandise and determining applicable duty rates. Use of the Service alone does not satisfy the reasonable care standard. Importers must independently verify all classifications and duty determinations through qualified professionals and official sources.

8. IEEPA Refund Estimator Disclaimer

8.1 Nature of Estimates

The IEEPA Refund Estimator provides rough approximations of duties that may be recoverable following judicial invalidation of IEEPA-based tariff actions. These estimates are based on general tariff rate schedules and User-provided inputs. They do not account for the specific classification of your goods, exclusions that may have applied, compound or specific duty rates, foreign trade zone treatment, drawback claims, or other factors that materially affect actual duty liability.

8.2 Evolving Legal Landscape

The legal framework governing IEEPA tariff refunds is subject to ongoing litigation, legislative action, and administrative rulemaking. Refund eligibility, procedures, deadlines, and recoverable amounts may change materially. The Service's estimates reflect our understanding of the law as of the date of calculation but do not account for subsequent legal developments.

8.3 No Guarantee of Refund

An estimate generated by the Service does not guarantee that you are entitled to a refund of any amount, that CBP will process any refund claim, or that your specific entries are eligible for relief. Refund recovery depends on numerous factors including entry liquidation status, protest filing deadlines (19 U.S.C. § 1514), applicable court orders, and the specific tariff provisions under which duties were assessed.
